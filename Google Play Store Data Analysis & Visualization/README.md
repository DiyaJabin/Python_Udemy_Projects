\# Day 76 – Google Play Store Data Analysis \& Visualization



Course project from a Udemy Python course.



\## Overview



This project performs an exploratory data analysis (EDA) of over 10,000 Android applications from the Google Play Store.



Using Pandas and Plotly, the dataset was cleaned, transformed, analyzed, and visualized to uncover trends related to app ratings, installs, pricing strategies, revenues, categories, genres, and market competition.



The goal was to practice real-world data analysis workflows, including data cleaning, feature engineering, aggregation, and interactive visualization.



\## Dataset



The dataset contains information about Android applications available on the Google Play Store, including:



\* App Name

\* Category

\* Rating

\* Reviews

\* Size

\* Installs

\* Type (Free/Paid)

\* Price

\* Content Rating

\* Genres



Dataset source provided as part of the course materials.

\## Insights Discovered



\* Most highly rated apps often have very few reviews, making ratings alone unreliable.

\* The majority of top-downloaded apps are free.

\* Free apps significantly outperform paid apps in total installs.

\* Certain categories dominate both downloads and competition.

\* Revenue potential varies considerably across app categories.

\* App pricing strategies differ widely depending on category and market segment.
